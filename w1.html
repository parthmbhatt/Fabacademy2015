Conceptualization and Scribbles

I recently stumbled upon this really cool game called Besiege and I thought to myself, why not create a catapult that actually aims for you.
The idea is simple, a shaft, that can rotate around the X and Z axes and that can fire a projectile with variable power

Functionality breakdown

The Base of the machine will house the motors and the board as well as provide a pivot for the rotting table
The channel will be hinged to the rotating table at one end and will tilt via a curved rack which will pass through a slot in the table ( the centres of the curved rack and the hinge have to coincide)
The rack will be driven by gear run by a stepper motor attached to the swiveling table
the table will swivel using a stepper motor mounted either at the center of the table (low speed/high torque required) or at the edge in which case the table an be one large gear and the stepper can run a smaller gear to obtain a desirable gear ratio (1:10?) to turn the table
the angle of the swivel table will be set by the user, along with the desired distance of travel and minimum height clearance, if any
The angle and power to be applied will be calculated using the equations of projectile motion, taking into consideration viscous drag on the projectile (ideal projectile would be a ball bearing)
As of now I have not frozen upon a design for applying variable power to the projectile, but I'm thinking of using a ferromagnetic material as the launching plug, attached to the spring, that is pulled back by switching on the electromagnet, different power levels would translate into corresponding spring compression
